Quadratic Equations

Quadratics can be defined as a polynomial equation of a second degree, which implies that it comprises a minimum of one term that is squared. It is also called quadratic equations. The general form of the quadratic equation is:
ax² + bx + c = 0
where x is an unknown variable and a, b, and c are numerical coefficients. For example, x² + 2x + 1 is a quadratic equation. Here, 'a' must not be equal to zero; otherwise, the equation becomes linear, such as:
bx + c = 0
Thus, this equation cannot be considered a quadratic equation.
The terms a, b, and c are also referred to as quadratic coefficients.
The solutions to the quadratic equation are the values of the unknown variable x, which satisfy the equation. These solutions are called roots or zeros of quadratic equations. The roots of any polynomial are the solutions to the given equation.

What is Quadratic Equation?

The polynomial equation whose highest degree is two is called a quadratic equation or sometimes just quadratics. It is expressed in the form of:
ax² + bx + c = 0
where x is the unknown variable and a, b, and c are constant terms.

Standard Form of Quadratic Equation

Since the quadratic equation includes only one unknown term or variable, it is called univariate. The power of variable x is always a non-negative integer. Hence, the equation is a polynomial equation with the highest power of 2.
The solutions for this equation are the values of x, also known as zeros. Zeros of the polynomial satisfy the equation. In the case of quadratics, there are two roots or zeros of the equation. When the values of roots or x are placed on the left-hand side of the equation, it equals zero. Therefore, they are called zeros.

Quadratic Formula

The formula for solving a quadratic equation is used to find the roots of the equation. Since quadratics have a degree equal to two, there are typically two solutions for the equation. For a quadratic equation ax² + bx + c = 0, the formula to find the roots is:
\ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\
The ± sign indicates two distinct solutions for x. Learn more about the quadratic formula here.

Examples of Quadratics

Beneath are the illustrations of quadratic equations of the form ax² + bx + c = 0:
  • x² – x – 9 = 0
  • 5x² – 2x – 6 = 0
  • 3x² + 4x + 8 = 0
  • -x² + 6x + 12 = 0
Examples of a quadratic equation with the absence of a 'C' - a constant term:
  • -x² – 9x = 0
  • x² + 2x = 0
  • -6x² – 3x = 0
  • -5x² + x = 0
  • -12x² + 13x = 0
  • 11x² – 27x = 0
Following are the examples of a quadratic equation in factored form:
  • (x – 6)(x + 1) = 0 [Result obtained after solving is x² – 5x – 6 = 0]
  • –3(x – 4)(2x + 3) = 0 [Result obtained after solving is -6x² + 15x + 36 = 0]
  • (x − 5)(x + 3) = 0 [Result obtained after solving is x² − 2x − 15 = 0]
  • (x – 5)(x + 2) = 0 [Result obtained after solving is x² – 3x – 10 = 0]
  • (x – 4)(x + 2) = 0 [Result obtained after solving is x² – 2x – 8 = 0]
  • (2x + 3)(3x – 2) = 0 [Result obtained after solving is 6x² + 5x – 6]
Below are examples of a quadratic equation with an absence of linear coefficient 'bx':
  • 2x² – 64 = 0
  • x² – 16 = 0
  • 9x² + 49 = 0
  • -2x² – 4 = 0
  • 4x² + 81 = 0
  • -x² – 9 = 0

How to Solve Quadratic Equations?

There are basically four methods of solving quadratic equations. They are:
  • Factoring
  • Completing the square
  • Using Quadratic Formula
  • Taking the square root

Factoring of Quadratics

To factor a quadratic equation of the form ax² + bx + c = 0:
  1. Ensure that the equation is set to zero.
  2. Factor the left-hand side of the equation, assuming zero on the right-hand side.
  3. Assign each factor equal to zero.
  4. Solve the equation to determine the values of x.
If the leading coefficient 'a' is not equal to one, a specific method is followed for arranging the factors.

Completing the Square Method

This method involves completing the square to solve quadratic equations. For example, to solve 2x² – x – 1 = 0:
  1. Move the constant term to the other side of the equation.
  2. Divide both sides by 2.
  3. Add the square of half of the coefficient of x to both sides.
  4. Factor the right side and solve for x.

Using Quadratic Formula

The quadratic formula can be used to find the roots of a quadratic equation. For a quadratic equation ax² + bx + c = 0:
x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / 2a
The ± sign indicates two distinct roots.

Taking the Square Root

This method is used for equations like x² + a² = 0. For example, to solve x² – 50 = 0:
  1. Move the constant term to the other side.
  2. Take the square root of both sides.
  3. Solve for x.

Conclusion

Quadratic equations can be solved using various methods, including factoring, completing the square, using the quadratic formula, and taking the square root. The quadratic formula is a common method, but other techniques can also be effective.
